Strategic Impact: Patient Adherence Predictive Framework
1. The Business Problem
In the specialty pharmaceutical market, patient non-adherence accounts for an estimated $30B in lost annual
revenue globally. For a high-value biologic brand, even a 1% improvement in "Days on Therapy" (DOT) can translate to $10M–$50M in incremental net revenue.

2. Analytical Solution
This project utilizes an XGBoost-based classification model to identify patients with a high probability of therapy discontinuation before it occurs.
Precision Targeting: Focuses nurse-support resources on the top 10% of at-risk patients.

   Explainable AI (SHAP): Provides commercial teams with the specific drivers (e.g., high out-of-pocket costs, low digital engagement)
   to personalize intervention messaging.

3. Estimated ROI & KPIs
By implementing this predictive framework, the organization can target the following strategic outcomes:
Revenue Retention: Projected 5–8% reduction in patient drop-offs, preserving brand equity.
Operational Efficiency: 20% improvement in Patient Support Program (PSP) resource allocation by moving from a "blanket" support model to a "risk-based" model.

   Improved Patient Outcomes: Longer duration on therapy leads to better clinical efficacy and long-term real-world evidence (RWE) data quality.

4. Scalability for GCCs
This framework is built with a modular architecture, allowing it to be scaled across multiple therapeutic areas (Oncology, Immunology, Cardio)
 by simply updating the feature engineering layer to reflect specific market dynamics.
